Biography

Bio

Carsten Rott is a professor in the Department of Physics & Astronomy at the University of Utah. He joined the university in 2021, where he also holds the Jack W. Keuffel Memorial Chair in Physics. Since August 2023, he has served as chair of the department. His research program centers on astroparticle physics and uses neutrinos as probes of the universe, with particular emphasis on high-energy neutrino astronomy, dark matter searches, and physics beyond the Standard Model.

Rott has long been involved in international collaborations connected to the IceCube Neutrino Observatory and related neutrino and dark matter experiments. His work links observational particle astrophysics with broader questions about cosmic accelerators, fundamental interactions, and even the composition of Earth’s interior.

Research

Research Interests

  • High-energy neutrino astronomy with the IceCube Neutrino Observatory.
  • Indirect and direct searches for dark matter signatures.
  • Physics beyond the Standard Model using neutrino data.
  • Multi-messenger studies connecting neutrinos with gamma rays and other cosmic messengers.
  • Neutrino oscillation and detector-based probes of Earth’s internal composition.
  • Experimental participation in IceCube, JSNS², COSINE, and Hyper-Kamiokande-related research.
Research Theme

The central thread of Rott’s work is the use of neutrinos to open new observational windows on the universe, from extreme astrophysical environments to fundamental particle interactions and the structure of our own planet.
